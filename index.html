About

Born in West Sussex, England and arriving in New Zealand early 2016 at the young age of 11, after 8 years in the Bay Of Plenty I relocated down to Dunedin to persue Graphic Design. At the beginning of 2024 I completed the Art and design Level 4 course. This portfolio will focus mostly on the photography-based work I developed during that course. Currently working through the Digital Media and Design Level 4 course, and developing photography skills even further, as well as working with Photoshop and Illustrator.

Unnamed

This was a self-negotiated project I did for the final course of my NZ Certificate in Art and Design Level 4. This came out as three A2 posters designed around current beauty standards. She could be seen admiring herself in the mirror in the third picture, holding her white peony flowers representing pure love, illumination, and beauty. The first image with her hands covering her face could be interpreted as not wanting any external sources of love other than her own. I developed two separate artworks from the photos from this photoshoot.

Unnamed

This was the second part of the self-negotiated project I did for the final course of my Certificate, and it became my showpiece for our final group exhibition. I designed the concept around current beauty standards I photographed my friend El with a series of different lighting, exploring with colour, angles and compositions.

Antibears

All of the images are of antibears I have designed. I use polymer clay for the faces, as well as sculpting tools to achieve the rough texture which represents fur.

I like to create and design these bears as a hobby during my free time, typically designing a personality before I sketch to ensure their appearance has a sense of individuality, bringing them to life.

Gemma, the bear above was designed as a gift to my mother, she represents a daring personality with a sense of curiosity within nature and a desire to learn.

Kyle, a strange looking bear with no voice. He is misunderstood, and represents a feeling of not belonging, as a result he does not look like the typical cute stuffed teddy bear.
This is something I personally can relate to, being neurodivergent and hard of hearing. I decided to reflect those feelings in his personality and appearance.

DevilCake is my first attempt at an antibear. I paid more attention to the details as I learnt how to manipulate polymer clay to achieve the textures and appearance that I wanted. As you can see; his eyelids are not as well-shaped and developed as Gemma's. Typically I would sew the faces onto the bears after removing their previous faces using either thread or wool.

Digital Media

These posters were developed for the Digital Media and Design Level 4 course. The task was to photograph a subject and create a gig poster for a fictional band or solo artist. I decided to photograph my friend, El, who is in a Riot grrrl band called Vagina Dry, based in Otepoti Dunedin. However, their solo artist name would be "Strawberry Jam", we decided to play into this role, and I ended up developing two gig posters using both Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator.
